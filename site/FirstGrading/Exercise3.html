How to Cook Pork Siomai

_

______ This is a quick and easy recipe for pork siomai. This is the recipe that I prefer the most because it is simple and straightforward. It also allows everone to enjoy their favorite siomai in 30 minutes or less.

WHAT IS SIOMAI?

______ Siomai is a type of Chinese dumpling. It can be made from different types of meat and vegetables with the addition of seasonings and spices. The ingredients are mixed altogheter and secured in a wrapper.

______ The Siomai that we will do is a Philippine version. We will use pork, a little bit of shrimp, along with egg and vegetables. The egg serves as the binder with the main function of holding all the ingredients together. It also prevents the mixture from crumbling. Siomai is usually cooked by steam, or through a method known as steaming. It is best eaten with a dip composed of soy sauce and calamansi and a spoonful of spicy chili garlic paste.

How to Cook Pork Siomai

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 1/2 lbs Ground Pork
  • 6 pieces Medium Shrimp
  • 1 medium White Onion
  • 1 medium Carrots
  • 1/2 cup Mushrooms
  • 1 large Egg
  • 1 1/2 tsp Sesame Oil
  • 1/2 tsp Ground Black Pepper
  • 2 tsp Salt
  • 1 pack Siomai Wrapper
    •

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Mince the carrot, onion, mushroom, and shrimp. Place all minced ingridents in a large mixing bowl.
  2. Add the ground pork, sesame oil, salt, pepper, and egg. Mix until all the ingredients are well blended.
  3. Scoop 1 to 1 1/2 tablespoons of the siomai mixture. Put it in the middle of a Siomai Wrapper. Secure while leaving the top open.
  4. Arrange the wrapped siomai into a steamer. Steam for 20 to 25 minutes.
  5. Create your sauce for dipping by mixing soy sauce and calamansi. You can also add chili paste and fried garlic to add more flavor.
  6. Your Pork Siomai is now finished and is ready to be served.
